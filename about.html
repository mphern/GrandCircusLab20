About Pete "Maverick" Mitchell

Bio

Growing up in a military family, I always knew I wanted to be a fighter pilot. My father, Duke Mitchell, was also a fighter pilot. He was shot down and killed during the Vietnam War when I was very young. I've been told that my father was the best fight pilot in the Navy so I worked my butt over to live up to his legacy. I've been told my piloting style is at times "dangerous and reckless" but I always put my plane and crew first. My hard work paid off when I was invited to the Top Gun program, a program where the best pilots compete to be the best of the best. Unfortunately during the program tragedy struck when my co-pilot, Nick "Goose" Bradshaw, was killed during an excercise due to a mulfunction during an ejection from the aircraft caused by an engine blowout. I went into a deep depression after that and I thought I would never be able to reach my full potential as a fighter pilot.

After the conclusion of the program. Me and my fellow Top Gun pilots were called into action to provide air support for a disabled aircraft carrier that had wandered into enemy waters. Pilots Tom "Iceman" Kazansky and Rick "Hollywood" Neven were first in the air. They encountered 5 MiG-28s which engaged them and Hollywood was shot down. I was then given clearance to take off and join the battle. During the fight, my engines nearly blowout again, the same way they did when Goose was killed. I was able to recover, but that ordeal brought back the terrible memories of the day I lost my best friend. I looked deep down inside and asked Goose to give me strength the continue the battle. Just then, I felt Goose's presence and I knew what I had to do. I re-engaged in the battle, worked my magic, and soon the battle was won.

After that day, I re-evaluated my life goals and decided that I wanted to become a Top Gun instructor and teach a new generation of fighter pilots how to become the best of the best.
